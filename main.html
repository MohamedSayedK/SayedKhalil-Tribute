Dr. Sayed Khalil

Senior Researcher / Associate Professor

Biography

Sayed Khalil was born in Cairo, Egypt in 1967. He received a Bachelor of Science degree in Biochemistry and Chemistry from Ain Shams University, Cairo, Egypt, in 1989. In 1991, he joined the National Institute of Oceanography and Fisheries (NIOF) and in 1993 he moved to the Agricultural Genetic Engineering Research Institute (AGERI), Ministry of Agriculture and Land Reclamation, Egypt. During his employment, he got his Master of Science degree in Biochemistry from Ain Shams University in 1998. In 1999, he joined North Carolina State University to study his Ph. D. In 2004, he got his Ph. D. in Entomology and Biotechnology under the direction of Dr. R. Michael Roe, Department of Entomology at NC State.

Experience and Skills

  • 1-Entomology and Acarology:

    Insect physiology and molecular biology. Insect juvenile hormone regulation and role in insect reproduction. Digestive system structure, function and gene expression in response to feeding. The role of tick synganglion, hormones and neuropeptides in tick reproduction and metamorphosis. Tick major storage proteins.

  • 2-Biotechnology and Molecular Biology:

    DNA and RNA isolation, Gene silencing using RNAi, Gene cloning, PCR techniques, Nucleic acid hybridization. Next generation sequencing such as Roche 454 and illumine sequencing. Bioinformatics: creating and managing sequence databases. Using sequence analysis software such as NCBI tool kit, Vector NTI and others.

  • 3-Cell culture and protein expression:

    Insect cell lines (SF9, High five, DM cells). Protein expression in bacterial and insect cells using different expression systems (stable transformation andBaculovirus expression). Purification and detection of proteins.

  • 4-Protein stabilization and delivery into insect bodies:

    Enhancing protein stability and movement through insect gut and cuticle by chemical modification for the purpose of production of safe insecticides.

Research Activities

  • - Physiological and molecular studies of insect digestion.

  • - Hormonal and molecular control of metamorphosis and reproduction in insects, mites and ticks.

  • - Transcriptomic of tick and mite sensory organs, nervous and reproductive system.

  • - Development of novel environmentally friendly pesticides.

  • - Production of genetically modified crops resistant to insects.

  • - Enhancing activity and stability of insecticidal proteins through chemical modification.
